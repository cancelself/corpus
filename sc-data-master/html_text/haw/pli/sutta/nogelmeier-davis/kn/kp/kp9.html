Karaṇīya Metta Sutta

Eia nā ʻano e pono ai ke hana Ka mea i hialoa i ka ʻike ʻi ʻo Ma muli o ka ʻike lihi i ke kūlana maluhia Me ia ka hiki, ka pono, a ke kūpono, Ke akahai, ka waipahē, a me ka ho ʻokano ʻole.

ʻOlu ʻolu nō i ka mea loa ʻa, Pili pono i ka hana a ma ʻalahi ka nohona. Mālie ka ʻike me ka lono, mahao ʻo nō, Me ka maha ʻoi ʻole a mākilo paha.

ʻA ʻole e hana i kahi mea iki A ka na ʻauao e ho ʻohalahala ai. E ʻolu a palekana mai ʻō a ʻō A ʻolu ka nohona o nā mea a pau.

E ʻolu nā mea ola Koe koena ʻole Ha ʻalulu a ʻonipa ʻa paha, Lō ʻihi, nunui, a waena ho ʻi, Pōkole, ʻu ʻuku, a kūāhewa nō…

E ʻolu nā mea a pau loa Inā ʻike ʻia a kīhēhē paha ʻO ko ʻō lā, a ko ʻone ʻi ho ʻi, Nā mea e ola nei, a e ʻō ʻili mai ana.

Mai ho ʻopunipuni kekahi i kekahi A ho ʻokano aku i kekahi wahi mea Mai mana ʻo hō ʻino aku iā ha ʻi No ka ukiuki me ke kuapu ʻe o loko.

Kohu makuahine e ho ʻopalekana loa ana I kāna keiki hānau kahi-me ka mōliaola Pēlā ke ʻano e mālama mau ai I nā mea ola a pau loa, a palena ʻole.

E mālama i ka lokomaika ʻi palena ʻole, No ke ao holo ʻoko ʻa ē; A i luna, i lalo, i ʻō, i ʻane ʻi, Me ka inaina a ho ʻokae ʻole E kōī ma ʻema ʻe wale aku ai.

Inā kū, a hele, a noho, a moe iho paha, ʻOiai ala maila ka no ʻono ʻo E kūpa ʻa mau kēia kau ʻana o nā maka Kapa ʻia he ʻano lani ihola, ʻānō nei ē.

Pa ʻa ʻole i ka ʻikena, kū i ka pono Me ke akāka le ʻa e kō pono ai A a ʻo ʻia iho ka make ʻe ʻole i ka le ʻa Kū ihola ke ko ʻu me ka hānau hou.

Translated by M. Puakea Nogelmeier and Jake H. Davis.

The translation, together with background notes and discussions, may be found on the Vipassana Hawai’i website.

M. Puakea Nogelmeier is a Professor Emeritus at U.H. Mānoa, where he taught Hawaiian language, history, and culture for 35 years. He is also the Executive Director of Awaiaulu, training translators in the arts of accessing the historical repository of Hawaiian language.

Jake H. Davis has trained as a meditation practitioner, scholar, monastic, and teacher in the Theravāda Buddhist tradition of Burma, and holds a doctorate in philosophy and cognitive science. He has held teaching and research positions at the City University of New York, Brown University, and New York University.
