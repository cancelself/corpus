• Dharmapada T210

19. Old Age

Verse 19.1What’s there to rejoice about, what’s there to laugh about?
Life is always in a blaze!
Deeply concealed in Darkness,
It’s better to seek light.

Verse 19.2Look at this physical body
Which [the fool] clings to as comfort.
It’s full of [false] thinking and causes sickness,
How does [the fool] know it’s unreality?

Verse 19.3When one gets old, the body becomes weak,
Sickness befalls and lustre is lost;
The skin slackens, and muscles contract.
One is fast approaching death.

Verse 19.4When the body dies, consciousness departs,
Like a driver abandoning a vehicle;
The flesh disappears, the bones scatter
What is there in the body to rely on?

Verse 19.5The body is like a city,
Framed with bones and plastered with the flesh.
From birth till old-age and death,
It stores nothing but hatred and conceit.

Verse 19.6When one gets old, the body changes,
Just like an old vehicle.
The Dharma can end suffering
—Practice it with diligence.

Verse 19.7If a man has heard no [wise teaching]
He will grow old to be like a bull:
With only well developed flesh,
But no wisdom.

Verse 19.8The cycle of birth and death is boring;
The coming and going is troublesome.
When one’s thoughts are attached to the body,
Endless suffering will follow.

Verse 19.9As the wise perceives suffering,
He accordingly abandons the body;
His thoughts are ceased and conditioning cut off;
And lust being ended, there is no more birth.

Verse 19.10He who neither practices the Brahmā-like faring,
Nor has acquired any wealth;
At old age, is like a white heron
Watching an empty pond.

Verse 19.11If one neither observes the precepts,
Nor accumulates wealth;
When old and weak and panting,
What’s the use of reminiscing the past?

Verse 19.12At old age, one is like autumn leaves;
One moves in filth, and wears tattered clothes.
Life is rapidly escaping from this birth;
There is no use to regret.

Verse 19.13Life is running out as days and nights pass.
Strive hard when there is still time.
What pertains to the world is truly impermanent,
Don’t be deluded and fall into darkness.

Verse 19.14Learn to kindle the mind lamp,
And train yourself to gain wisdom.
Move away from taints, don’t get stained;
Holding the [mind] candle, watch the stages of progress.
