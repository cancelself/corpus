27. The Spiritually Committed

Verse 27.1He who is committed to the spiritual path
Does not compete for profits.
Regarding the profitable and the unprofitable,
He is desireless and undeluded.

Verse 27.2He who is ever kind and ready to learn,
Who courses with a righteous mind
Embracing the precious [Dharma] at heart, wise;
Is indeed one cultivating the path.

Verse 27.3One becomes a wise one
Not necessarily by arguing much.
He who is secure and fearless
And guards his goodness, is indeed wise.

Verse 27.4One is an upholder of the Dharma
Not simply by speaking much.
He who, though not having heard much
Puts the Dharma into practice himself
And who forgets not the Teaching
May be called a Dharma uholder.

Verse 27.5One becomes an elder
Not simply by being old,
With ripened body and grey hairs.
Such a person [can] be just a fool.

Verse 27.6He in whom are truth, Dharma,
Restraint and kindness;
Who is wise and pure,
Is indeed an Elder.

Verse 27.7One becomes graceful
Not by having flower-like complexion
While being miserly, jealous and deceitful,
And acting contrary to his words.

Verse 27.8He who can renounce evil
Having cut it off by its root,
Who is wise and hatred-free
Is said to be graceful.

Verse 27.9One becomes a śramaṇa
Not merely by shaving one’s head
While still telling lies and coveting,
Full of desires like the ordinary people.

Verse 27.10He who can stop doing evil
Who generously propagates the Teaching
Who stills his mind and thoughts
Is indeed a śramaṇa.

Verse 27.11One becomes a bhikṣu
Not merely by begging at times
While having evil acts and sexual indulgence
This is bhikṣu by name only.

Verse 27.12He who abandoning bad and good
being pure, leads the spiritual life
Who is wise and destroys evil,
Is indeed a bhikṣu.

Verse 27.13One becomes a sage
Not by [mere] silence,
Having impure intentions wihtin
And pretending externally.

Verse 27.14He whose mind attaches to naught,
Who courses contentedly within
Having attained quiescence here and beyond
Is indeed a sage.

Verse 27.15One is said to be spiritually advanced
Not [simply] by saving one life
He who strives for universal salvation,
Harmless, is indeed spiritually advanced.

Verse 27.16Do not boast of your observances,
[Or that] “My actions are mostly truthful.”
[Or that] “I have attained mental concentration
By resorting to solitude.”

Verse 27.17Be determined to seek happiness;
Don’t learn from the ordinary worldlings.
So long as your corruptions are not extinct
You will not be liberated.
