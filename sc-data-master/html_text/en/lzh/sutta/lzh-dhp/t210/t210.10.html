• Dharmapada T210

10. Heedlessness

Verse 10.1The ethical life is the way to immortality;
Heedlessness is the Path to death.
Those without craving never die;
To lose the Way is to lose one’s life.

Verse 10.2The wise excels in guarding the Way
And never becomes heedless.
Not craving, one attains to happiness;
Whence the bliss of attainment is obtained.

Verse 10.3One should constantly devote to meditation,
Be firm, and persevere in the proper course.
The strong ones thereby transcend existence,
And [attain Nirvāṇa], the Supremely Auspicious.

Verse 10.4One who always gives rise to right mindfulness,
And whose actions are pure;
His evil will be easily eradicated,
Self-restrained, living by Dharma,
Not transgressing, skillful, his name spreads afar.

Verse 10.5Bringing forth effort and being heedful,
Restraining himself and taming his mind,
The wise makes an illuminator [for himself],
And does not return to the sea of darkness.

Verse 10.6It is difficult to understand the fools’ mind;
It is greedy, scattered and delights in disputes
The intelligent man values caution,
And protects it as his precious wealth.

Verse 10.7Do not crave or delight in disputes,
And do not indulge in sensual pleasure.
He who practices meditation and heedfulness,
Can gain great happiness.

Verse 10.8To be heedless is like to be self-imprisoned.
He who realizes this is a noble one,
Who has ascended to wisdom’s mansion,
Free from danger, truly safe.
Such a wise man looks down upon the foolish,
Like [one] on the mountain and [the other] on the ground.

Verse 10.9Reigning upright amidst the chaotic,
One is uniquely awakened.
Such a man’s power surpasses a lion’s.
Leaving evil, he becomes the Greatly Wise.

Verse 10.10By drowsiness, heavy as a mountain;
The deluded one is covered.
Lying down comfortably, he acknowledges no suffering,
And hence enters the womb again and again.

Verse 10.11There can be no occasion for self-indulgence;
He who is well-restrained can exhaust the cankers.
The moment when one indulges, Mara gets his chance,
Like a lion [waiting] to catch the deer.

Verse 10.12He who has no self-indulgence,
Is a disciplined bhikṣu.
He, whose thoughts are well calmed
Should constantly guard his mind.

Verse 10.13A bhikshu finds happiness in caution;
Heedlessness gives him much worry.
Changing disputes from small to big,
And accumulating evil, he gets himself into fire.

Verse 10.14Precept observance is meritorious and brings happiness;
Transgressing the precepts produces fear in the heart.
Destroying the cankers of the three spheres.
One comes close to Nirvāṇa.

Verse 10.15If one was heedless earlier,
But regains self-control later
—Such a one illuminates the world.
*Being mindful, he is focused on proper [actions].

Verse 10.16If one made mistakes in doing evil,
But later covers over it by doing good
—Such a one illumines the world.
*Being mindful, he becomes skillful in proper [actions].

Verse 10.17If one abandons the home-life when young and strong
To vigorously practice the Buddha’s teachings
—Such a one illumines the world,
Like the moon freed from clouds.

Verse 10.18If one previously had committed evil
But later stops and commits no more;
—Such a one illumines the world,
Like the moon freed from clouds.

Verse 10.19He who when living does naught to cause pain,
And at death does not grieve;
Has seen the Truth, and is fearless.
He feels no sorrow where [others] do.

Verse 10.20He who can eliminate the black dharmas,
And trains only in the pure white ones;
Will cross over deep water, never to return.
He moves about with no attachment,
Unsullied by [sensual] pleasure,
With no craving, and no sorrow.
