11. Mind

Verse 11.1Mind rushes to echoes,
It is difficult to guard, to restrain.
The wise straightens its base,
And its brightness then becomes great.

Verse 11.2[Mind] is fickle, hard to take care of
It complies with every desire.
To subdue the mind is excellent
One’s mind tamed, there will be calm.

Verse 11.3[Mind] travels alone, going far off,
It moves according with craving.
The wise always protects his own mind;
When it is guarded, there will be peace.

Verse 11.4[Mind] travels alone, going far off,
Is concealed and incorporeal;
Those who restrain this mind, drawing near the way,
Will be released from Mara’s bonds.

Verse 11.5If the mind does not settle down,
Or know the Dharma;
And is deluded in worldly affairs;
There will be no proper understanding.

Verse 11.6Thoughts do not stop where they should;
They are ceaseless and endless.
Meritorious actions can halt evil,
He who is aware [of this] is noble.

Verse 11.7Though the mental events taught by the Buddha,
Are subtle and not [ultimately] real;
One must beware of the indulgent thoughts,
And not let the mind go as it likes.

Verse 11.8Seeing the Dharma, one is happiest;
One’s aims will be fulfilled.
The wise guards the subtle thoughts,
And breaks the causal conditions for suffering.

Verse 11.9The body cannot be had for long,
It will return completely to the ground;
Body decayed and consciousness departed
—What can one covet as a boarder therein?

Verse 11.10The mind strives to build dwelling places,
To come and go endlessly.
[If] one’s thoughts are mostly devious,
One incurs troubles for oneself.

Verse 11.11It is one’s own mind which causes [suffering]
Not one’s father or mother.
One must then work hard to align with the right,
And not turn back from meritorious actions.

Verse 11.12Conceal the six [senses] like a tortoise.
Guard the mind like [the army guarding] the capital,
Fight Māra with wisdom;
And if victorious, there will be no more trouble.
