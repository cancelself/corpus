• Dharmapada T210

31. The Elephant Simile

Verse 31.1I, like an elephant in battle
Unafraid of being struck by arrows,
Ever with sincere faith
Transform those without virtues.

Verse 31.2Just as a properly trained elephant
Is fit for the king to ride on;
One who is trained is exalted among men
And fit to be believed in.

Verse 31.3Even those as properly tamed,
As the thoroughbred horses of Sindh,
And the best elephants;
Cannot escel a man who has tamed himself.

Verse 31.4Those [untrained men] cannot go
To places where men have not been
Only those who have subdued themselves
Can go to the place of the subdued.

Verse 31.5Like an elephant named Dhanapālaka,
Who is ferocious and hard to restrain;
Though chianed and not given food
It still remains wild as a rutted elephant.

Verse 31.6Those immersed in evil actions,
Ever have themselves chained by greed;
Like the elephant which is insatiable,
And thus repeatedly enter into the womb.

Verse 31.7Formerly, the mind has been drifting aimlessly,
And always going where it finds pleasure.
[Now, I] shall give it all up and subdue passions,
Like a mahout subduing an elephant with hooks.

Verse 31.8He who delights in the Path and is heedful,
Who can constantly protect his mind
Is one who lifts himself out of suffering;
Like an elephant getting out of a trap.

Verse 31.9If you get a capable companion
Who would move with you, and is firm in doing good;
You will defeat all the famous
And remain mindful having attained your Goal.

Verse 31.10If you don’t get a capable companion
Who would move with you and is firm in doing good;
Then dissociate from all, [like] a king his lands.
It’s better to be alone without doing evil.

Verse 31.11It’s better to move alone, doing good;
And not befriend the fools.
Be alone and do no evil,
Like a frightened elephant protecting itself.

Verse 31.12Happiness is to be born with wealth.
Happiness is to have gentle and agreeable friends.
Happiness is having merit at life’s end.
Happiness is not committing any evil.

Verse 31.13Happiness is having a mother in the family.
Happiness too is having a father.
Happiness is the existence of śramaṇas in the world.
Happiness is the existence of the Way in the world.

Verse 31.14Happiness is practicing virtue till old age.
Good is when one’s faith is pure where it should be pure.
Wisdom delights oneself most.
The greatest is not doing evil.

Verse 31.15Just as a horse that is properly trained,
Goes according to the tamer’s wish;
One endowed with faith, virtue, vigour,
Concentration and examination of dharmas,

Verse 31.16Who is established in knowledge and conduct,
Who has patience and mental stability,
Will get rid of all suffering,
In accordance with his wish.

Verse 31.17From this, one will go into concentration,
Like a horse having been tamed,
One is freed from anger and the corruptions,
And experiences the bliss of gods.

Verse 31.18He who is not heedless
will thereby become more wakeful,
Like a nag compared to a fine horse:
He who gives up evil is the better.
