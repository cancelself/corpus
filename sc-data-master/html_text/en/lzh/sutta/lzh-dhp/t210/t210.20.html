• Dharmapada T210

20. Self Concern

Verse 20.1He who has love for himself
Should consciously protect what’s being guarded,
He should aspire for the release from craving
And, forgoing sleep, to be rectified.

Verse 20.2Oneself comes first,
One should train hard oneself,
When benefit is derived, then instruct others.
The wise is thus vexed.

Verse 20.3First learn to rectify oneself,
Then only rectify others.
Train yourself to enter wisdom,
And you will surely move upwards.

Verse 20.4If one cannot benefit oneself,
How can one benefit others?
When one’s mind is tamed, and body rectified,
What aim is there that cannot be achieved?

Verse 20.5What has been done by oneself before,
One will experience it’s effect later.
Having done evil one [suffers] through oneself,
Like a diamond boring through a gem.

Verse 20.6If one does not observe the precepts,
[Craving] will spread a like a rattan creeper.
One’s desires being unrestrained,
Evil actions will increase day by day.

Verse 20.7Evil actions endanger oneself;
They are easy for the fool.
Good actions are one’s best security;
They are difficult for the fool.

Verse 20.8Those who accord with the True Men’s teaching,
And live in accordance with the Truth,
The fool envies them,
And views them as evil.

Verse 20.9Evil actions yield bad [results],
Like the sowing of bitter seeds.
Having done evil, one suffers oneself;
Having done good, one experiences happiness oneself.
And each [result] must ripen [accordingly]
—It cannot be [experienced] on each other’s behalf.
[Like-wise], good actions yield good [results],
Like the sowing of sweet seeds.

Verse 20.10One should benefit oneself as well as others,
And there will be benefit without much effort.
If one desires to know how to benefit oneself,
[Here it is]: Precepts and knowledge are the best.

Verse 20.11One who has love for himself,
And wishes to be reborn in heavens,
Should, with respect and delight, listen to the Dharma
And remember the Buddha’s Teachings.

Verse 20.12One must think carefully before any undertaking,
So that one’s effort must not be spoilt.
Thus one’s thinking becomes more and more trained,
And one does not miss the time for any undertaking.

Verse 20.13One who has his undertaking well prepared,
Can reach the goal and achieve profit.
A true understanding should be put into practice,
And one will thereby achieve one’s aim.
