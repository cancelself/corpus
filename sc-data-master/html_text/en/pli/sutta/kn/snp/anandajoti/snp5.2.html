The Young Man Ajita’s Questions

SC 1SC Verse 1039SVV 5.57PTS Verse 1032SVSV 5.2.1“By what is the world enveloped?” said venerable Ajita,
“Why does it not become clear?
What do you say is its defilement? What is the world’s great fear?”

SC 2SC Verse 1040SVV 5.58PTS Verse 1033SVSV 5.2.2“The world is enveloped by ignorance, Ajita,” said the Gracious One,
“because of heedlessness and meanness it does not become clear.
Hunger is its defilement, I say; suffering is the world’s great fear.”

SC 3SC Verse 1041SVV 5.59PTS Verse 1034SVSV 5.2.3“Streams are flowing everywhere,” said venerable Ajita,
“What is the constraint for streams?
Tell me the restraint for streams; by what are the streams shut off?”

SC 4SC Verse 1042SVV 5.60PTS Verse 1035SVSV 5.2.4“Whatever streams there are in the world, Ajita,” said the Gracious One,
“mindfulness is the constraint for them.
That is the restraint for streams, I say; by wisdom they are shut off.”

SC 5SC Verse 1043SVV 5.61PTS Verse 1036SVSV 5.2.5“Wisdom and also mindfulness,” said venerable Ajita,
“and mind and body, dear Sir,
please tell me this when asked: where does this cease?”

SC 6SC Verse 1044SVV 5.62PTS Verse 1037SVSV 5.2.6“This question that was asked, Ajita, I can answer it! As to where mind and body ceases without remainder:
with the cessation of consciousness, in this place it ceases.”

SC 7SC Verse 1045SVV 5.63PTS Verse 1038SVSV 5.2.7“Those who have discerned the Teaching, and the many in training here,
when I ask the prudent one, please tell me their conduct, dear Sir.”

SC 8SC Verse 1046SVV 5.64PTS Verse 1039SVSV 5.2.8“He should not be greedy for sense pleasures, or be disturbed in mind.
Skilful in all things, the monk should wander mindfully.”

The Young Man Ajita’s Questions are Finished
