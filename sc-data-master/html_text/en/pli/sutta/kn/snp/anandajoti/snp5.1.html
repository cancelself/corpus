SC 1SC Verse 983SVV 5.1PTS Verse 976SVSV 5.1.1From the delightful city of the Kosalans to the Southern lands went

SC 2SC Verse 984SVV 5.2PTS Verse 977SVSV 5.1.2In the locality of Assaka, and near to Mūḷaka

SC 3SC Verse 985SVV 5.3PTS Verse 978SVSV 5.1.3In the vicinity of the river there was a large village,
and with the income that arose from that he offered a great sacrifice.

SC 4SC Verse 986SVV 5.4PTS Verse 979SVSV 5.1.4When that great sacrifice had been given he entered his hermitage again,
and when he had re-entered it, another brahmin came along—

SC 5SC Verse 987SVV 5.5PTS Verse 980SVSV 5.1.5he had sore feet, and was thirsty, with dirty teeth, and dust on his head—
and after approaching the first brahmin, he begged for five hundred coins.

SC 6SC Verse 988SVV 5.6PTS Verse 981SVSV 5.1.6After seeing him Bāvari invited him to take a seat,
and he asked after his happiness and welfare, and this is the word he spoke:

SC 7SC Verse 989SVV 5.7PTS Verse 982SVSV 5.1.7“Whatever kind of gift I had, all that has been given away by me,
please excuse me brahmin, I do not have the five hundred.”

SC 8SC Verse 990SVV 5.8PTS Verse 983SVSV 5.1.8“If your honour will not give me what I am begging for,
then within seven days may your head split into seven pieces!”

SC 9SC Verse 991SVV 5.9PTS Verse 984SVSV 5.1.9Having made a scene the dishonest man proclaimed this fearful thing.
After hearing this word of his, Bāvari became miserable.

SC 10SC Verse 992SVV 5.10PTS Verse 985SVSV 5.1.10Going without food he dried up, and was affected by the dart of grief,
and when his heart was like that his mind did not delight in meditation.

SC 11SC Verse 993SVV 5.11PTS Verse 986SVSV 5.1.11Having seen him terrified and miserable, a God who desired his welfare,
approached Bāvari, and this is the word he spoke:

SC 12SC Verse 994SVV 5.12PTS Verse 987SVSV 5.1.12“He does not know the head—he is a dishonest man who wants wealth!
He does not have knowledge about the head or head-splitting.”

SC 13SC Verse 995SVV 5.13PTS Verse 988SVSV 5.1.13“But now your honour knows! Please explain this to me when asked:
the head and head-splitting, may we hear that word of yours.”

SC 14SC Verse 996SVV 5.14PTS Verse 989SVSV 5.1.14“I also do not know about this, I do not have this knowledge here.
The head and head-splitting! Indeed only Victors see this!”

SC 15SC Verse 997SVV 5.15PTS Verse 990SVSV 5.1.15“Then who knows about the head and head-splitting
on the face of the earth? O God, please explain this to me.”

SC 16SC Verse 998SVV 5.16PTS Verse 991SVSV 5.1.16“From out of the city of Kapilavatthu a world leader has renounced,
he is of king Okkāka’s line, a Sakyan’s son, a light-maker.
He is a Sambuddha, brahmin, in everything he is perfect,

SC 17SC Verse 999SVV 5.17PTS Verse 992SVSV 5.1.17“Having attained all deep knowledges and strengths, endowed with Vision regarding all things,
he has come to the end of all actions, in the end of all clinging he is freed.

SC 18SC Verse 1000SVV 5.18PTS Verse 993SVSV 5.1.18“He is the Buddha in the world, the Gracious One, the Visionary who teaches Teaching,
after going there you can ask him about it, he will explain it to you.”

SC 19SC Verse 1001SVV 5.19PTS Verse 994SVSV 5.1.19Having heard the word ‘Sambuddha’, Bāvari became elated,
then his grief became but little, and he received great happiness.

SC 20SC Verse 1002SVV 5.20PTS Verse 995SVSV 5.1.20Bāvari, uplifted and elated,
enthusiastically asked that God:
“In which village, or again in which town,
in which country, is the Lord of the World?
Where having gone to can we revere the Sambuddha, the Supreme Human Being?”

SC 21SC Verse 1003SVV 5.21PTS Verse 996SVSV 5.1.21“The Victor is in the Kosalan’s city, Sāvatthī,
he has much wisdom, excellent and great intelligence,
that Sakyan’s son, who is free from burden and pollutant,
that Bull of a Man has understanding of head-splitting.”

SC 22SC Verse 1004SVV 5.22PTS Verse 997SVSV 5.1.22So then the brahmin addressed his students, who were perfect in the Vedas:
“Come, young men, and I shall explain, please listen to this word of mine:

SC 23SC Verse 1005SVV 5.23PTS Verse 998SVSV 5.1.23“He whose manifestation in the world is always exceptional,
has now arisen in the world, renowned as one called a ‘Sambuddha’.
Having gone quickly to Sāvatthī, you can see the Supreme Human Being.”

SC 24SC Verse 1006SVV 5.24PTS Verse 999SVSV 5.1.24“Having seen him how will we know that he is the ‘Buddha’, O brahmin?
You must tell us who do not know, how we can know it is him.”

SC 25SC Verse 1007SVV 5.25PTS Verse 1000SVSV 5.1.25“There has come down to us in the Vedas the marks of a Great Man,
they are explained as thirty-two, complete in regular order.

SC 26SC Verse 1008SVV 5.26PTS Verse 1001SVSV 5.1.26“For whoever has on his limbs the marks of a Great Man,
there can be just two destinies, for a third cannot be found:

SC 27SC Verse 1009SVV 5.27PTS Verse 1002SVSV 5.1.27“If he lives in a house, when he has been victorious over this earth,
without a stick or a sword he will rule according to the Teaching.

SC 28SC Verse 1010SVV 5.28PTS Verse 1003SVSV 5.1.28“But if he goes forth from the house to the houseless life,
he becomes a Cover-Remover, a Sambuddha, a Worthy One, unexcelled.

SC 29SC Verse 1011SVV 5.29PTS Verse 1004SVSV 5.1.29“About my birth, family, and marks; lore, students and other things,
about the head and head-splitting, you must ask in your mind.

SC 30SC Verse 1012SVV 5.30PTS Verse 1005SVSV 5.1.30“If he should be a Buddha, one who sees without obstruction,
the questions that are asked in your mind he will answer by way of speech.”

SC 31SC Verse 1013SVV 5.31PTS Verse 1006SVSV 5.1.31After hearing Bāvari’s words, the sixteen brahmin students:
Ajita, Tissa Metteyya, Puṇṇaka, also Mettagū,

SC 32SC Verse 1014SVV 5.32PTS Verse 1007SVSV 5.1.32Dhotaka, and Upasīva, Nanda, and also Hemaka,
the two: Todeyya and Kappa, and Jatukaṇṇī, the one who is wise,

SC 33SC Verse 1015SVV 5.33PTS Verse 1008SVSV 5.1.33Bhadrāvudha and Udaya, and also the brahmin Posāla,
Mogharāja, the intelligent one, and the great seer, Piṅgiya,

SC 34SC Verse 1016SVV 5.34PTS Verse 1009SVSV 5.1.34each one with his own group, renowned throughout the whole world,
meditators delighting in meditation, wise ones, influenced by their pre-dispositions,

SC 35SC Verse 1017SVV 5.35PTS Verse 1010SVSV 5.1.35after worshipping Bāvari, and circumambulating him,
they all, wearing matted-hair and deer-skins, set out for the North:

SC 36SC Verse 1018SVV 5.36PTS Verse 1011SVSV 5.1.36From Mūḷaka to Patiṭṭhāna first, then on to Mahissati,
to Ujjenī, and Gonaddha, to Vedisa, and to the place called Vanasa,

SC 37SC Verse 1019SVV 5.37PTS Verse 1012SVSV 5.1.37to Kosambī, and Sāketa, and Sāvatthī, the city supreme,

SC 38SC Verse 1020SVV 5.38PTS Verse 1013SVSV 5.1.38to Pāva, to Bhoganagara, to Vesāli, and to the Magadhan city Rājagaha,

SC 39SC Verse 1021SVV 5.39PTS Verse 1014SVSV 5.1.39Just like a thirsty man to cool water, or like a trader to great gain,
like one scorched by the heat to shade, quickly he climbed the mountain.

SC 40SC Verse 1022SVV 5.40PTS Verse 1015SVSV 5.1.40Now at that time the Gracious One was in front of the Community of monks,
teaching the Teaching to the bhikkhus, roaring like a lion in a forest,

SC 41SC Verse 1023SVV 5.41PTS Verse 1016SVSV 5.1.41Ajita saw the Sambuddha, brilliant like the hundred-rayed sun,
like the moon which has reached fullness on the fifteenth day of the month,

SC 42SC Verse 1024SVV 5.42PTS Verse 1017SVSV 5.1.42and after seeing on his limbs the characteristics in their fullness,
standing cheerfully on one side he asked a question in his mind:

three marks are on his limbs, he is perfect in the three Vedas.

SC 45SC Verse 1027SVV 5.45PTS Verse 1020SVSV 5.1.45“In the marks, and the traditions, with the glossaries, and the ritual,

SC 46SC Verse 1028SVV 5.46PTS Verse 1021SVSV 5.1.46“Your investigation of Bāvari’s marks, O One Supreme among Men,
O craving-cutter, make that clear, so that we are not left doubting.”

SC 47SC Verse 1029SVV 5.47PTS Verse 1022SVSV 5.1.47“He can cover his face with his tongue, there is hair between his eyebrows,
