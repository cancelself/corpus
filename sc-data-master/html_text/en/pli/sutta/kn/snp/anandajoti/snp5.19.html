Verses on the Recital of the Way to the Beyond

SC 1SC Verse 1138SVV 5.156PTS Verse 1131SVSV 5.19.1“I will recite the Way to the Beyond,” said venerable Piṅgiya,
“as he saw it, so he explained: unstained, with great intelligence,
the one free from sense desire, the unentangled one, the Strong One, why should he speak falsely?

SC 2SC Verse 1139SVV 5.157PTS Verse 1132SVSV 5.19.2“Of the one who gave up stain and delusion, who gave up conceit and malevolence,
come now, I will proclaim this lyric which is endowed with beauty:

SC 3SC Verse 1140SVV 5.158PTS Verse 1133SVSV 5.19.3“The Buddha, the darkness-dispeller, the All-Seeing Visionary,
who has gone to the end of the world, transcended all of existence
given up all suffering, the one pollutant-free,
whose very name is truth—O brahmin, you are worshipped by me.

SC 4SC Verse 1141SVV 5.159PTS Verse 1134SVSV 5.19.4“Just as a bird who has given up a barren woodland,
might live in a forest which has much fruit,
so too I have given up those of little wisdom,
like a swan who has arrived at the great sea.

SC 5SC Verse 1142SVV 5.160PTS Verse 1135SVSV 5.19.5“Those who explained things to me in the past, before Gotama’s teaching,
saying ‘it was so, and so it will be’, all that was just hearsay,
all that just increased my thoughts.

SC 6SC Verse 1143SVV 5.161PTS Verse 1136SVSV 5.19.6“Alone sits the Darkness-Dispeller, the Bright One, the Light-Maker,
Gotama of Great Knowledge, Gotama of Great Intelligence.

SC 7SC Verse 1144SVV 5.162PTS Verse 1137SVSV 5.19.7“He who taught the Teaching to me, which is visible, not subject to time,
the end of craving, beyond calamity, for which there is nowhere a likeness.”

SC 8SC Verse 1145SVV 5.163PTS Verse 1138SVSV 5.19.8Bāvari: “Then why do you dwell apart from him, even for a second, Piṅgiya,
from Gotama of Great Knowledge, Gotama of Great Intelligence,

SC 9SC Verse 1146SVV 5.164PTS Verse 1139SVSV 5.19.9“He who taught the Teaching to you, which is visible, not subject to time,
the end of craving, beyond calamity, for which there is nowhere a likeness?”

SC 10SC Verse 1147SVV 5.165PTS Verse 1140SVSV 5.19.10“I would not dwell apart from him even for a second, brahmin,
from Gotama of Great Knowledge, Gotama of Great Intelligence,

SC 11SC Verse 1148SVV 5.166PTS Verse 1141SVSV 5.19.11“He who taught the Teaching to me, which is visible, not subject to time,
the end of craving, beyond calamity, for which there is nowhere a likeness.

SC 12SC Verse 1149SVV 5.167PTS Verse 1142SVSV 5.19.12“I see him in my mind as though with the eye,
as I dwell heedful night and day, brahmin.
“Revering him I make the night pass by,
for that reason I think there is no real dwelling apart.

SC 13SC Verse 1150SVV 5.168PTS Verse 1143SVSV 5.19.13“My confidence, happiness, mind, and mindfulness,
do not depart from Gotama’s teaching.
Whatever direction the One of Great Wisdom goes to
it is that very place that I am inclined.

SC 14SC Verse 1151SVV 5.169PTS Verse 1144SVSV 5.19.14“I am old, without strength and feeble,
because of that my body does not go there,
but by an effort of thought I always go,
for my mind, Brahmin, is joined to him.

SC 15SC Verse 1152SVV 5.170PTS Verse 1145SVSV 5.19.15“Lying down in a quagmire I was trembling,
I drifted from island to island,
then I saw the Sambuddha, the flood-crosser, the one pollutant-free.”

SC 16SC Verse 1153SVV 5.171PTS Verse 1146SVSV 5.19.16The Buddha said: “Just as Vakkali released his confidence—
and Bhadrāvudha, and Āḷavi-Gotama—
just like that you must release your confidence,
and you will go, Piṅgiya, beyond the realm of Death.”

SC 17SC Verse 1154SVV 5.172PTS Verse 1147SVSV 5.19.17“I am more and more sure about this, having heard the sage’s word,
the cover-remover, the Sambuddha, who is unhindered, assured in speech,

SC 18SC Verse 1155SVV 5.173PTS Verse 1148SVSV 5.19.18“Who knows the great gods, who has understood everything far and near,
the Teacher who makes an end to questions in those who have doubts, and make them known.

SC 19SC Verse 1156SVV 5.174PTS Verse 1149SVSV 5.19.19The Indestructible, the Unagitated, for which there is nowhere a likeness,
Surely I will go there, about this I have no doubt,
thus bear me in mind as one whose heart is intent on Nibbāna.”

The Discourse on Piṅgiya, the Sixteenth

The Way to the Beyond is Finished
