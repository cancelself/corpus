3.9. To Vāseṭṭha on Who is a Brahmin

SC 1Thus have I heard:

SC 2At one time the Radiant One was dwelling at Icchānaṅgala. Now at that time a number of notable and prosperous brahmins were staying at Icchānangala, that is to say the brahmins Caṅkī, Tārukkha, Pokkharasāti, Jāṇussoni and Todeyya, as well as other notable and prosperous brahmins.

SC 3Then, as the young brahmins Vāseṭṭha and Bhāradvaja were walking and wandering for exercise this subject of discussion arose between them. “How is one a brahmin?” The young brahmin Bhāradvaja said, “When one is well-born on both sides, of pure maternal and paternal descent through seven generations in the past, then one is a brahmin”.

SC 4But Vāseṭṭha the young brahmin said, “When one is virtuous and fulfils one’s vows, then one is a brahmin”. Bhāradvaja could not convince Vāseṭṭha while the latter failed to convince the former.

SC 5Then Vāseṭṭha said to Bhāradvaja, “Sir, the samaṇa Gotama son of the Sakyas who left home from the Sakyan clan is living at Icchānaṅgala, in the forest near Icchānaṅgala. Now the good reputation of Master Gotama has spread in this way: ‘That Radiant One is accomplished, completely Awakened, possessed of True Knowledge and conduct, well-gone for himself and others, knower of the worlds, unexcelled trainer of those who can be tamed, teacher of devas and humanity, Awake and Radiant.’ Come, Bhāradvaja, let us go to the samaṇa Gotama and ask him about this. As he replies, so will we bear his words in mind.”

SC 6“Yes, sir”, Bharadvaja replied.

SC 7So the two young brahmins approached the Radiant One and exchanged greetings with him. When this courteous and amiable talk was concluded, they sat down to one side, and the young brahmin Vāseṭṭha addressed the Radiant One in verse:

SC 8SC Verse 600Vāseṭṭha
Of Pokkharasāti the pupil I am,
while student of Tārukkha is he;
both of us have acknowledged mastery
in the threefold Veda lore.

SC 9SC Verse 601We have attained totality
over all the Vedic masters teach;
as philologists, grammarians,
and we chant as our masters do.

SC 10SC Verse 602The subject of “birth”, O Gotama,
is contention’s cause with us:
he, a Bhāradvāja, does declare
“birth” is due to brahmin caste,
while I say it’s by karma caused:
know it thus, O One-with-Eyes.

SC 11SC Verse 603Sir, to ask about this we have come,
to you acclaimed as Wide Awake,
each of us unable is
the other to convince.

SC 12SC Verse 604As they raise their lotussed hands
towards the moon waxed full,
so to you, by this world revered,
we pay homage too.

SC 13SC Verse 605So now of Gotama the Eye
uprisen in the world, we ask:
Is one by “birth” a brahmin,
or a brahmin karma-caused?
Explain to us who do not know
how we should “brahmin” recognize?

SC 14SC Verse 606Buddha
I shall analyse for you,
in order due and as they are,
the types of “birth” ’mong living things,
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 15SC Verse 607First, there’s grasses and the trees,
though of themselves they nothing know,
each species possessing its own marks,
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 16SC Verse 608Next come beetles, butterflies,
and so on to the termites, ants,
each species possessing its own marks,
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 17SC Verse 609Then, know of those four-footed kinds,
both the tiny and the huge,
each species possessing its own marks
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 18SC Verse 610Know those whose bellies are their feet,
that is, the long-backed group of snakes,
each species possessing its own marks,
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 19SC Verse 611Know too the many kinds of fish,
living in their watery world,
each species possessing its own marks,
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 20SC Verse 612Then know the varied winged ones,
the birds that range the open skies,
each species possessing its own marks,
for many are the sorts of birth.

SC 21SC Verse 613While in those births are differences,
each having their own distinctive marks,
among humanity such differences
of species—no such marks are found.

SC 22SC Verse 614Neither in hair, nor in the head,
not in the ears or eyes,
neither found in mouth or nose,
not in lips or brows.

SC 23SC Verse 615Neither in neck, nor shoulders found,
not in belly or the back,
neither in buttocks nor the breast,
not in groin or sexual parts.

SC 24SC Verse 616Neither in hands nor in the feet,
not in fingers or the nails,
neither in knees nor in the thighs,
not in their “colour”, not in sound,
here is no distinctive mark
as in the many other sorts of birth.

SC 25SC Verse 617In human bodies as they are,
such differences cannot be found:
the only human differences
are those in names alone.

SC 26SC Verse 618’Mong humankind whoever lives
by raising cattle on a farm,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as farmer not as Brahmin then.

SC 27SC Verse 619’Mong humankind whoever lives
by trading wares here and there,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as merchant not as Brahmin then.

SC 28SC Verse 620’Mong humankind whoever lives
by work of many arts and crafts,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as craftsman not as Brahmin then.

SC 29SC Verse 621’Mong humankind whoever lives
by serving other’ needs and wants,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as servant not as Brahmin then.

SC 30SC Verse 622’Mong humankind whoever lives
by taking things that are not given,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as a thief not Brahmin then.

SC 31SC Verse 623’Mong humankind whoever lives
by the skill of archery,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as soldier not as Brahmin then.

SC 32SC Verse 624’Mong humankind whoever lives
by performing priestly rites,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as a priest not Brahmin then.

SC 33SC Verse 625’Mong humankind whoever lives
through enjoying towns and lands,
O Vāseṭṭha you should know
as rajah not as Brahmin then.

SC 34SC Verse 626Him I call not a brahmin, though
born from brahmin mother’s line,
if with sense of ownership,
he’s just supercilious:
owning nothing and unattached—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 35SC Verse 627Who fetters all has severed,
who trembles not at all,
gone beyond ties, free from bonds—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 36SC Verse 628Having cut strap and reins,
the rope and bridle too,
and tipped the shafts, as one Awake—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 37SC Verse 629Who angerless endures abuse,
beating and imprisonment,
with patience-power, an arméd might—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 38SC Verse 630Who’s angerless and dutiful,
of virtue full and free of lust,
who’s tamed, to final body come—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 39SC Verse 631Like water drop on lotus leaf,
or mustard seed on needle point,
whoso clings not to sense desires,
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 40SC Verse 632Here who comes to Know
exhaustion of all dukkha,
laid down the burden, free from bonds—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 41SC Verse 633Skilled in the Path, what’s not the path,
in wisdom deep, sagacious one,
having attained the highest aim—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 42SC Verse 634Not intimate with those gone forth,
nor with those who dwell at home,
without a shelter, wishes few—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 43SC Verse 635Who has renounced all force
towards all being weak and strong,
who causes not to kill, nor kills—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 44SC Verse 636Among the hostile, friendly,
among the violent, cool,
detached amid the passionate—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 45SC Verse 637From whoever lust and hate,
conceit, contempt have dropped away,
as mustard seed from needle-point—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 46SC Verse 638Who utters speech instructive,
true and gentle too,
who gives offence to none—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 47SC Verse 639Who in the world will never take
what is not given, long or short,
the great or small, the fair or foul—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 48SC Verse 640In whom there are not longings found
for this world or the next,
longingless and free from bonds—
on such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 49SC Verse 641In whom is no dependence found,
with Final Knowledge, free from doubt,
duly wont to the Deathless deeps—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 50SC Verse 642Here who’s gone beyond both bonds:
to goodness and to evil too,
one who’s sorrowless, stainless, pure—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 51SC Verse 643Vanished is all love of being,
like the moon—unblemished, pure,
that one serene and undisturbed—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 52SC Verse 644Who’s overpassed this difficult path,
delusion’s bond, the wandering-on,
who’s crossed beyond, contemplative,
craving not, no questions left,
no clinging’s fuel, so Cool become—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 53SC Verse 645Who has abandoned sense desires,
as homeless one renouncing all,
desire for being all consumed—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 54SC Verse 646Who has abandoned craving here
as homeless one renouncing all,
craving for being all consumed—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 55SC Verse 647Abandoned all the human bonds
and gone beyond the bonds of god
unbound one is from every bond—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 56SC Verse 648Abandoned boredom and delight,
become quite cool and assetless
A hero, All-worlds conqueror,
one such I say’s a Brahmin then

SC 57SC Verse 649Whoever knows of being’ death,
their being born in every way,
unshackled, faring well, Awake—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 58SC Verse 650Whose destination is unknown
to humans, spirits or to gods,
pollutions faded, Arahat—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 59SC Verse 651For whom there is not ownership
before or after or midway,
owning nothing and unattached—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 60SC Verse 652One noble, most excellent, heroic too,
the great sage and the one who conquers all,
who’s faultless, washes, one Awake—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 61SC Verse 653Who knows their former births
and sees the states of bliss and woe
and then who wins the waste of births—
one such I say’s a Brahmin then.

SC 62SC Verse 654Whatever’s accepted and “name” and “clan”
is just a worldly designation—
by conventions handed down
accepted everywhere.

SC 63SC Verse 655But those asleep, unquestioning,
who take up views, who do not Know,
unknowingly they’ve long declared:
one’s a brahmin just by “birth”.

SC 64SC Verse 656One’s not a brahmin caused by “birth”,
nor caused by “birth” a non-brahmin;
a brahmin’s one by karma caused,
by karma caused a non-brahmin.

SC 65SC Verse 657By karma caused a farmer is,
one’s a craftsman karma-caused,
by karma caused a merchant is,
one’s a servant karma-caused.

SC 66SC Verse 658By karma caused a robber is,
one’s a soldier karma-caused,
by karma caused a priest becomes,
one’s a ruler karma-caused.

SC 67SC Verse 659Thus according as it is
people wise do karma see;
Seers of causal relatedness,
skilled in karma, its results.

SC 68SC Verse 660Karma makes the world go on,
people by karma, circle round;
sentient beings are bound to karma,
as a cart is pulled by a horse.

SC 69SC Verse 661By ardour and the Good Life leading,
with restraint and taming too:
by this a Brahmin one becomes,
one’s by this a Brahmin best.

SC 70SC Verse 662Possessed of Triple Knowledges,
at Peace, rebirth come to an end—
know Vāseṭṭha, such a one
is Brahmā and Sakra for those who Know.

SC 71When this was said the young brahmins Vāseṭṭha and Bhāradvāja exclaimed to the Radiant One: “Magnificent, Master Gotama! The Dharma has been clarified by Master Gotama in many ways, as though he was lighting what was overthrown revealing what was hidden, showing the way to one who was lost, or holding a lamp in the dark so that those with eyes can see forms. We go for refuge to Master Gotama, to the Dharma and to the Saṅgha. May Master Gotama remember us as upāsakas who from today have Gone for Refuge for life.
