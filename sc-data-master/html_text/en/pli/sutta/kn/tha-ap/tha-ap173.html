171. Kumudamāliya

Walt 2104 In the Himalayan Mountains,
there was a large, natural lake.
I was a rakkhasa born there,
of frightful form, having great strength. Verse 1

Walt 2105 White lotuses were blooming there,
arising just as big as wheels,
and I then picked those lotuses.
The Strong One’s group was there then too. Verse 2

Walt 2106 But Atthadassi, Blessed One,
the Biped-Lord, the Bull of Men,
seeing that those flowers were picked,
did come into my presence then. Verse 3

Walt 2107 The God of Gods, the Bull of Men,
the Sambuddha approached me then.
Picking up all of those flowers
I gave them all to the Buddha. Verse 4

Walt 2108 That company then stretched out to
the ends of the Himalayas.
With a canopy over him
the Thus-Gone-One did journey forth. Verse 5

Walt 2109 In the eighteen hundred aeons
since I offered that flower to him,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that’s the fruit of Buddha-pūjā. Verse 6

Walt 2110 In the fifteenth aeon ago,
there were seven lords of people,
wheel-turning kings with great power,
all known as Sahassaratha. Verse 7

Walt 2111 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 8

Thus indeed Venerable Kumudamāliya Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Kumudamāliya Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
