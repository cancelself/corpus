119. Paccupaṭṭhānasaññaka

Walt 1748 Right when Well-Gone Atthadassi
had achieved final nirvana,
I was born in a spirit’s womb;
I possessed great glory back then. Verse 1

Walt 1749 “It’s a bad attainment for me,
a bad dawn, a bad arising,
that I should find such opulence
when the Eyeful One’s passed away.” Verse 2

Walt 1750 Discerning what I was thinking,
the follower named Sāgara
with a wish for my upliftment,
then came into my presence there: Verse 3

Walt 1751 “Why are you grieving? Do not fear!
o you knower of the Teaching,
by the Buddha have been given
the seeds of everyone’s success. Verse 4

Walt 1752 He who’d worship the Sambuddha,
Siddhattha, Leader of the World,
should worship even one small bone
after the final nirvana. Verse 5

Walt 1753 When the heart’s pleasure is the same
there is the same priceless merit.
Therefore having built a stupa,
worship the Victor’s relics there.” Verse 6

Walt 1754 After hearing Sāgara’s words,
I then built a Buddha-stupa.
For five years I attended to
that Sage’s ultimate stupa. Verse 7

Walt 1755 Due to that deed for the Biped-Lord,
the World’s Best One, the Bull of Men,
having enjoyed great happiness,
I attained my arahantship. Verse 8

Walt 1756 In the seventh aeon ago
there were four named Bhūripañña,
wheel-turning kings with great power,
possessors of the seven gems. Verse 9

Walt 1757 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 10

Thus indeed Venerable Paccupaṭṭhānasaññaka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Paccupaṭṭhānasaññaka Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
