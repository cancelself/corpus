89. Bodhisaññaka

Walt 1542 There was a great Bodhi festival
for the Blessed One Vipassi.
I attended that festival,
being a person who’d renounced. Verse 1

Walt 1543 Taking water strewn with flowers
I poured it on the Bodhi Tree.
“Freed, he will make all of us free;
Gone Out, he will make us go out.” Verse 2

Walt 1544 In the ninety-one aeons since
I watered that Bodhi Tree then,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
the fruit of Bodhi-perception. Verse 3

Walt 1545 When thirty-three aeons had passed
there were eight wheel-turning kings,
lords over men who were all then
known as Udakāsecana. Verse 4

Walt 1546 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 5

Thus indeed Venerable Bodhisaññaka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Bodhisaññaka Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
