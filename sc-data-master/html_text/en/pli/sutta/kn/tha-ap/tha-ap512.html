507. {510.} Nāḷikeradāyaka

Walt 5448 In the city, Bandhumatī,
I worked in a hermitage then.
I saw the Spotless One, Buddha,
who was traveling through the sky. Verse 1

Walt 5449 Having taken a coconut,
I gave it to the Best Buddha.
Standing in the sky, the Calm One,
the Great Famed One accepted it. Verse 2

Walt 5450-5451 With a mind that was very clear,
having given Buddha that fruit,
productive of delight for me,
bringing happiness in this world,
I then came to possess great joy
and vast, ultimate happiness.
A gem was truly produced for
me, being reborn here and there. Verse 3-4

Walt 5452 In the ninety-one aeons since
I gave the Buddha fruit back then,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that is the fruit of giving fruit. Verse 5

Walt 5453 The divine eye is purified;
I’m skilled in meditative states.
Special knowledges perfected:
that is the fruit of giving fruit. Verse 6

Walt 5454 My defilements are now burnt up;
all new existence is destroyed.
Like elephants with broken chains,
I am living without constraint. Verse 7

Walt 5455 Being in Best Buddha’s presence
was a very good thing for me.
The three knowledges are attained;
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 8

Walt 5456 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 9

Thus indeed Venerable Nāḷikeradāyaka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Nāḷikeradāyaka Thera is finished.

The Summary:

Kaṇikār’,and Ekapatta,
Kāsumārī, thus Āvaṭa,
Pāra and Mātuluṅga too,
Ajela, also Amora,
Tāla and thus Nāḷikera:
the verses that are counted here
number just one hundred verses,
avoiding any less or more.

The Kaṇikāra Chapter, the Fifty-First
