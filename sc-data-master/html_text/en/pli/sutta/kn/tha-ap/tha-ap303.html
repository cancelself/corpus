301. Padumakesariya

Walt 2717 I lived in the Sage-assembly,
a fierce mātaṅga elephant.
Feeling pleasure for the Sages
I sprinkled some lotus pollen. Verse 1

Walt 2718 Having pleased my heart among those
Best of Self-Enlightened Victors,
Devoid of Passions, Neutral Ones,
I joyed an aeon in heaven. Verse 2

Walt 2719 In the ninety-one aeons since
I sprinkled that pollen back then,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that’s the fruit of flower-pūjā. Verse 3

Walt 2720 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 4

Thus indeed Venerable Padumakesariya Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Padumakesariya Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
