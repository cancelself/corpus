292. Pupphadhāraka

Walt 2676 I was a bark-clothed ascetic,
wearing deer-leather outer robes.
I had five special knowledges;
a person who could stroke the moon. Verse 1

Walt 2677 Seeing Vipassi, the World’s Torch,
come to the same place as me,
I bore the floral canopy
over the head of the Teacher. Verse 2

Walt 2678 In the ninety-one aeons since
I bore that floral canopy,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that’s the fruit of bearing flowers. Verse 3

Walt 2679 In the eighty-seventh aeon
hence, there was one lord of the earth
by name Samantadharaṇa,
a wheel-turning king with great strength. Verse 4

Walt 2680 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 5

Thus indeed Venerable Pupphadhāraka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Pupphadhāraka Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
