314. Sattapadumiya

Walt 2781 I was a brahmin, Nesāda,
dwelling on a riverbank then.
I swept out the hermitage there,
with lotus flowers as my broom. Verse 1

Walt 2782 Seeing the Golden Sambuddha,
Siddhattha, Leader of the World,
traveling through the forest once
I felt a feeling of delight. Verse 2

Walt 2783 Going to meet the Sambuddha,
Siddhattha, Leader of the World,
I brought him to the hermitage
and spread lotuses and lilies. Verse 3

Walt 2784 In the ninety-four aeons since
I did pūjā with that flower,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that’s the fruit of Buddha-pūjā. Verse 4

Walt 2785 In the seventh aeon ago
there were four Pādapāvaras,
wheel-turning monarchs with great strength,
possessors of the seven gems. Verse 5

Walt 2786 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 6

Thus indeed Venerable Sattapadumiya Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Sattapadumiya Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
