316. Āsanatthavika

Walt 2793 Sikhi Buddha, Supreme Stupa,
was the Kinsman of the World then.
I was journeying in a dark
forest, a barren wilderness. Verse 1

Walt 2794 After exiting the forest,
I saw the Buddha’s lion throne.
Confidently pressing my hands,
I praised the Leader of the World. Verse 2

Walt 2795 Throughout the entire day I praised
the Buddha, the World’s Chief Leader.
Happy, and with a happy heart,
I uttered this speech at that time: Verse 3

Walt 2796 “Praise to you, O Well-Bred Person!
Praise to you, Ultimate Person!
You’re the Omniscient, Great Hero,
the World’s Best, the Bull among Men.” Verse 4

Walt 2797 After praising Sikhi like that,
after saluting his throne there,
led away by other concerns,
I departed facing north. Verse 5

Walt 2798 In the thirty-one aeons since
I praised the Best Debater then,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that’s the fruit of singing praises. Verse 6

Walt 2799 In the twenty-seventh aeon
ago were seven Atulyas,
wheel-turning monarchs with great strength,
possessors of the seven gems. Verse 7

Walt 2800 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 8

Thus indeed Venerable Āsanatthavika Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Āsanatthavika Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
