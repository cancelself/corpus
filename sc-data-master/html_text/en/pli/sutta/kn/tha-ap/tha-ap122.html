120. Jātipūjaka

Walt 1758 When Vipassi Buddha was born,
there was a vast effulgence there.
The earth itself quaked in response,
as did its oceans and mountains. Verse 1

Walt 1759 They interpreted the omens:
“A Buddha is born in the world,
the Top of all living beings
who will lift up all the people.” Verse 2

Walt 1760Having heard news of the omens,
I performed pūjā for the birth.
There is no pūjā quite like the
pūjā that is done for the birth. Verse 3

Walt 1761 Having brought my own heart pleasure,
restraining myself wholesomely,
after doing that birth-pūjā
I passed away right on the spot. Verse 4

Walt 1762 In whichever womb I’m reborn,
whether it’s human or divine,
I surpass all other creatures:
that is the fruit of birth-pūjā. Verse 5

Walt 1763 The relics now look after me,
through the power of my own heart.
Others cannot disturb me now:
that is the fruit of birth-pūjā. Verse 6

Walt 1764 In the ninety-one aeons since
I performed that pūjā back then,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that is the fruit of birth-pūjā. Verse 7

Walt 1765 In the thirtieth aeon ago,
four and thirty lords of people,
all named Supāricariya,
were wheel-turning kings with great strength. Verse 8

Walt 1766 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 9

Thus indeed Venerable Jātipūjaka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Jātipūjaka Thera is finished.

The Summary:

Parivāra, Sumaṅgalya,
Saraṇ, Āsana, Pupphika,
Citapūjī, Buddhasaññī,
Magg’, Upaṭṭhāna, Jātina
ninety uttered verses are
counted clearly in this part.

The Mahāparivāra Chapter, the Twelfth.
