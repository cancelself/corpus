Walt 396 The snake-gods, titans and Brahmās
according to powers and strengths,
made offerings to the laid-out
mother who was in nirvana. Verse 170

Walt 397 In front were led off all of the
Well-Gone-One’s heirs in nirvana,
Gotamī was led off after,
honored wet-nurse of the Buddha. Verse 171

Walt 398 With the gods and people out front,
the snake-gods, titans and Brahmās,
and next, with followers, Buddha,
processed to worship his mother. Verse 172

Walt 399 The Buddha’s final nirvana
was not of such a kind as this.
Gotamī’s final nirvana
was extremely miraculous. Verse 173

Walt 400 The Buddha and monks won’t be seen
at Buddha’s final nirvana.
The Buddha is at Gotamī’s;
so’s Sāriputta and so on. Verse 174

Walt 401 Then they built the funeral pyres,
made with all sorts of fragrant wood,
and sprinkled with perfumed powder.
Those great nuns were cremated there. Verse 175

Walt 402 The remaining portions and bones
were completely consumed by fire.
And at that time Ānanda spoke
this speech, which was very moving: Verse 176

Walt 403 “Gotamī’s gone without a trace
and her corpse has been cremated,
intimating that the Buddha’s
nirvana too will soon occur.” Verse 177

Walt 404 Ānanda, urged by the Buddha,
placed Gotamī’s sacred relics
in her begging bowl at that time,
and presented them to the Lord. Verse 178

Walt 405-406 Taking them up with both his hands,
the Seventh Sage, the Buddha, said:
“Just as the trunk of a standing,
gigantic timber-bearing tree,
impermanent, breaks into bits,
however massive it may be,
so Gotamī, who was a nun,
has reached her final nirvana. Verse 179-180

Walt 407 O! it is a marvelous thing!
My mother who’s reached nirvana,
leaving only relics behind
did not grieve and was not wailing. Verse 181

Walt 408 Grieving not for others left,
she’s crossed the sea of existence.
She’s cooled, she’s in nirvana.
her torment is now avoided. Verse 182

Walt 409 Know this about her, O you monks,
she was a very wise woman,
with wisdom which was vast and wide,
distinguished among Buddhist nuns. Verse 183

Walt 410 She’d mastered the superpower
called the “divine ear” element.
Gotamī was a master of
the knowledge stored in others’ hearts. Verse 184

Walt 411 She remembered her former lives;
her “divine eye” was purified.
All the defilements were destroyed;
she will not be reborn again. Verse 185

Walt 412 She had purified her knowledge
of meaning and of the Teaching,
etymology and preaching:
because of that she did not grieve. Verse 186

Walt 413 A rod of iron that’s beaten
when it is glowing due to fire
slowly cools off, leaving no ash:
like that it’s not known where she went. Verse 187

Walt 414 No rebirth place can be discerned
of the truly liberated,
who cross the flood of lustful bonds,
who’ve reached unshaking happiness. Verse 188

Walt 415 Therefore be lamps unto yourselves;
graze in the field of mindfulness.
With wisdom’s seven parts attained,
you all should end your suffering. Verse 189

Thus indeed Bhikkhunī Mahāpajāpatīgotamī spoke these verses.

The legend of Mahāpajāpatīgotamī Therī is finished.
