Happiness

SC 197Happy indeed we live, friendly amidst the hostile. Amidst hostile men we dwell free from hatred.

SC 198Happy indeed we live, friendly amidst the afflicted (by craving). Amidst afflicted men we dwell free from affliction.

SC 199Happy indeed we live, free from avarice amidst the avaricious. Amidst the avaricious men we dwell free from avarice.

SC 200Happy indeed we live, we who possess nothing. Feeders on joy we shall be, like the Radiant Gods.

SC 201Victory begets enmity; the defeated dwell in pain. Happily the peaceful live, discarding both victory and defeat.

SC 202There is no fire like lust and no crime like hatred. There is no ill like the aggregates (of existence) and no bliss higher than the peace (of Nibbana).

SC 203Hunger is the worst disease, conditioned things the worst suffering. Knowing this as it really is, the wise realize Nibbana, the highest bliss.

SC 204Health is the most precious gain and contentment the greatest wealth. A trustworthy person is the best kinsman, Nibbana the highest bliss.

SC 205Having savored the taste of solitude and peace (of Nibbana), pain-free and stainless he becomes, drinking deep the taste of the bliss of the Truth.

SC 206Good is it to see the Noble Ones; to live with them is ever blissful. One will always be happy by not encountering fools.

SC 207Indeed, he who moves in the company of fools grieves for longing. Association with fools is ever painful, like partnership with an enemy. But association with the wise is happy, like meeting one’s own kinsmen.

SC 208Therefore, follow the Noble One, who is steadfast, wise, learned, dutiful and devout. One should follow only such a man, who is truly good and discerning, even as the moon follows the path of the stars.

Ācāriya Buddharakkhita, The Dhammapada: The Buddha’s Path of Wisdom, Buddhist Publication Society, 1985. (Revised, 1996).
