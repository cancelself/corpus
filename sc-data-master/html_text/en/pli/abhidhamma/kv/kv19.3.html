19.3 Of the Fruits of Life in Religion

Controverted Point: That the fruit of recluseship is unconditioned.

Theravādin: PTS cs 19.3.1Do you then identify that “fruit” with Nibbāna: the Shelter, the Cave, the Refuge, the Goal, the Past-Decease, the Ambrosial? Or are there two “unconditioneds”? You deny both alternatives but you must assent to one or the other. If to the latter, I ask are they both … Nibbānas, and is there one higher than the other,… or is there a boundary … an interstice between them?

PTS cs 19.3.2Again, do you imply that recluseship itself is unconditioned? “No, conditioned”, you say. Then is its fruit or reward conditioned? … .

PTS cs 19.3.3–4You admit, again, that the four stages in the recluse's Ariyan Path—the Four Paths—are conditioned. Yet you would deny that the Four Fruits are conditioned!

PTS cs 19.3.5In fact, you would have in these four and Nibbāna five “unconditioneds”. Or if you identify the four with Nibbāna, you then get five sorts of Nibbāna, five Shelters, and so on … .
