• Compendium of States or Phenomena

1.1. Mātikā of Triplets

  1. States that are good, bad, indeterminate.PTS p M1
  2. States that are associated with pleasant feeling, painful feeling, neutral feeling.
  3. States that are results; that have resultant quality; that are neither.
  4. States that are grasped at and favourable to grasping; that are not grasped at but are favourable to grasping; that are neither.
  5. States that are vitiated and vicious; that are not vitiated but are vicious; that are neither.
  6. States that have applied and sustained thinking; sustained thinking only; neither.
  7. States that are accompanied by zest; by happiness; by indifference.
  8. States that are to be put away by vision; by culture; by neither.
  9. States, the moral roots of which are to be put away by vision; by culture; by neither.
  10. States going to building up; going to pulling down; going to neither.
  11. States of one in training; of the adept; of one who is neither.
  12. States that are limited, sublime, infinite.
  13. States that have a limited object; a sublime object; neither.
  14. States that are base; of medium worth; excellent.
  15. PTS p M2States that are of a wrongfulness fixed as to consequences; that are of a righteousness fixed as to consequences; that do not entail fixed consequences.
  16. States that have the Path as object; whose moral root is the Path; whose dominant influence is the Path.
  17. States that have arisen; that have not arisen; that are bound to arise.
  18. States that are past; present; future.
  19. States that have the past as their object; the present as their object; the future as their object.
  20. States that belong to one's self; are external to one's self; are belonging or external to one's self.
  21. States that have for an object one's self; an object external to one's self; an object that is both.
  22. States that are visible and reacting; invisible and reacting; neither.

Here end the triplets.
