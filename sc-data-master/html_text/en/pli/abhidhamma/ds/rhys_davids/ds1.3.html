• Compendium of States or Phenomena

1.3. Suttanta

  • States that partake of wisdom; do not partake of wisdom.
  • States that resemble lightning; are comparable to the thunderbolt.
  • States that are foolish; discreet.
  • States that are dark; bright.
  • States that conduce to remorse; that do not.
  • States that are equivalent terms; processes of such.
  • PTS p M8States that are explanations; processes of explanation.
  • States that are expressions; processes of expression.
  • Name and shape.
  • Ignorance and craving for rebirth.
  • Theory of rebirth and theory of dissolution.
  • Theory of eternalism and theory of annihilation.
  • Theory of infiniteness and theory of finiteness.
  • Theory of first and last things.
  • Unconscientiousness and indiscretion.
  • Conscientiousness and discretion.
  • Contumacy and friendship with evil.
  • Suavity and friendship with good.
  • Skill in offences and in restoration from the offences.
  • Skill in the attainments and in recovery from the attainments.
  • Skill in the elements and in attention.
  • Skill in the spheres of sense and in the causal law.
  • Skill in affirming and in negating causal relation.
  • Upright and soft.
  • Patience and loveableness.
  • Amity and courtesy.
  • Unguardedness in the gateways of sense and immoderation in diet.
  • Guardedness in the gateways of sense and moderation in diet.
  • Forgetfulness and unintelligence.
  • Mindfulness and intelligence.
  • Computing power and developing power.
  • Calm and intuition.
  • PTS p M9The sign of calm and the sign of grasp.
  • Grasp and balance.
  • Moral failure and theoretic fallacy.
  • Moral achievement and theoretic achievement.
  • Purity of morals and of theory.
  • Purity in views and the struggle of him who holds the views.
  • Agitation on occasions calling for agitation and the struggle of the agitated.
  • Discontent as to good states and unfalteringness in the struggle.
  • Wisdom and freedom.
  • Knowledge in making an end; knowledge in not coming to pass.
