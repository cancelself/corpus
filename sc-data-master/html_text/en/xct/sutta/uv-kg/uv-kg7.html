7. Virtuous Conduct

Rock 1He who casts away wickedness in the body for a virtuous bodily course of life will be secure in his body if he is guarded against the great sins of the body.

Rock 2He who casts away wickedness in speech for virtuous speech will be secure in his speech if he is guarded against the great sins of speech.

Rock 3He who has cast away wickedness in thought for virtuous thoughts will be secure in his thoughts if he is guarded against the great sins of thought.

Rock 4He who has cast away wickedness in the body, who has cast away wickedness in speech, who has cast away wickedness in (his) thoughts, has cast away likewise all other stains.

Rock 5He who does what is virtuous in the body, he who is virtuous in his speech, he who is virtuous in his thoughts, will possess the four immeasurables.

Rock 6He who is virtuous in body, speech, and mind, obtains unceasing happiness here and in the other world.

Rock 7The sage whose body is well controlled can be harmed by nothing; he goes to an immortal dwelling-place, where there is no sorrow.

Rock 8The sage whose speech is well controlled can be harmed by nothing; he goes to an immortal dwelling-place, where there is no sorrow.

Rock 9The sage whose thoughts are always controlled can be harmed by nothing; he goes to an immortal dwelling-place, where there is no sorrow.

Rock 10The steadfast control their bodies, the steadfast control their speech, the steadfast control their minds; when the steadfast are well controlled in everything, they go to an immortal dwelling-place, where there is no sorrow.

Rock 11It is good to control the body, it is good to control the tongue (speech), it is good to control the mind; to have everything controlled is good: the Bhixu who is entirely controlled is freed from all sorrows.

Rock 12Watching over his speech, his mind well controlled, doing nothing that is evil with his body, by observing these three ways of doing one finds the way spoken by the Rischi.

Chapter on Virtuous Conduct, the Seventh.
