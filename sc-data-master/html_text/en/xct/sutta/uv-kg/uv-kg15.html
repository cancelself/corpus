15. Reflection (Smriti)

Rock 1It is the teaching of the Buddha, that whoever (commences by) reflecting on the operation of breathing, and who goes on through the different stages (of meditation), his mind well composed, will reach perfection, as the sun and moon, when free from clouds, illuminate the whole world.

Rock 2He who, standing, sitting, and sleeping, keeps both body and mind under control, such a thoughtful and well-controlled Bhixu will find the above-mentioned and other blessings; and if he has obtained the above-mentioned and the other blessings, he will not go to where is the lord of death.

Rock 3He who continually reflects on what appertains to the body, and has well under control the six senses (āyatanas), and who is always well composed, knows the extinction of sorrow.

Rock 4He who continually reflects on what appertains to the body in all its different shapes, on being without self, without attachment for the “mine,” will not care for self; he will have no attachment for the “mine:” in this manner will the Bhixu swiftly cross over from the regions of desire.

Rock 5He who is thoughtful, wise, well composed, happy, and pure, and who attends carefully at all times to this law, will, I declare, cross over from birth and old age.

Rock 6Thus learning to be always on his guard, the wise, thoughtful, and diligent Bhixu, after having cast off all bonds, finds by this means the destruction of sorrow (nirvāṇa).

Rock 7They who are awake can watch better than they who sleep, I tell you; it is better to watch than to sleep, for he who watches has no fear.

Rock 8He who is watchful and diligent is safe day and night, and he will find the end of pain (kleśā) in the longed-for cessation of death (nirvāṇa).

Rock 9They who day and night are reflecting on the Buddha, and who go to the Buddha for a refuge, these men have the profits of mankind.

Rock 10They who day and night are reflecting on the law (dharma), and who go to the law for a refuge, these men have the profits of mankind.

Rock 11They who day and night are reflecting on the church (sangha)), and who go to the church for a refuge, these men have the profits of mankind.

Rock 12The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on the Buddha.

Rock 13The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on the law.

Rock 14The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on the church.

Rock 15The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on the laws of morality (śīla).

Rock 16The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on renunciation.

Rock 17The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on the way.

Rock 18The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night reflecting on what appertains to the body.

Rock 19The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night keeping in mind the four kinds of meditation (samādhi).

Rock 20The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in the cessation of death.

Rock 21The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in kindness of heart.

Rock 22The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in meditation.

Rock 23The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in the unconditioned.

Rock 24The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in the uncharacteristic.

Rock 25The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in solitude.

Rock 26The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in what leads to salvation.

Rock 27The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night delighting in nothing (worldly?).

Rock 28The disciple of Gautama is always well awake, day and night rejoicing in nirvāṇa.

Chapter on Reflection, the Fifteenth.
